⌥ Open Science · advocacy

An open practice.

“I am an avid advocate for Open Science.” “Together, we can do better!”

Open Science is the practice of doing research in the open — data, code, methods, and reasoning shared so that the field can audit and build on what we publish. Below: ODIN, the community I help convene around open neurophysiology data; essays where I argue for the practice; and papers where I work the argument out.

ODIN (Open Data In Neuroscience) is an initiative I help organise out of the McGovern Institute at MIT, originally with DANDI, and from 2025 jointly with the Allen Institute for Neural Dynamics. ODIN convenes a community — experimentalists, theorists, infrastructure builders — around the standards, tools, and culture needed to share neurophysiology data across scales and modalities.
